Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery was founded in 1986 to protect sea turtles from extinction. The hatchery collects turtle eggs from the beaches and keeps them in a protected area until they hatch. The hatchlings are then released into the ocean when they are strong enough to survive.

The hatchery also treats injured turtles and cares for those that cannot survive in the wild due to injuries or disabilities. Visitors can observe the hatchery's work and learn about sea turtle conservation.

Plan Your Visit

  • Location: Hikkaduwa, Galle District, Southern Province, Sri Lanka
  • Hours: 8am-6pm daily
  • Admission: Adults LKR 500, Children LKR 250
  • Contact: +94 77 654 1123, hikkaduwahatchery@gmail.com

Visitors are welcome to visit the hatchery and learn about sea turtle conservation efforts. Guided tours are available, and visitors can even participate in releasing baby turtles into the ocean!
