Threats to Turtles

Sea turtles face many threats to their survival, including:

  • Loss of nesting habitat due to development and erosion
  • Predation of eggs and hatchlings by animals like raccoons and dogs
  • Entanglement in fishing gear, such as nets and lines
  • Ingestion of marine debris, like plastic bags and balloons, which can cause blockages in their digestive systems and death
  • Overharvesting for their meat, shells, and eggs, which are considered delicacies in some cultures
  • Climate change, which can affect sea turtle nesting patterns and the availability of food

It is important that we work to mitigate these threats to protect sea turtles and their habitats.

Conservation Efforts

Many organizations, including the Sri Lankan government and local hatcheries, are working to protect sea turtles and their habitats. Conservation efforts include:

  • Protecting nesting habitats through laws and regulations
  • Creating artificial nesting sites, like hatcheries, to protect eggs and hatchlings
  • Developing fishing gear that is less likely to entangle sea turtles
  • Conducting research to better understand sea turtle biology and behavior
  • Educating the public about the importance of sea turtle conservation
